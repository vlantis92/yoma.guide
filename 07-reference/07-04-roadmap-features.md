# Roadmap Features

*Last updated: February 2026*

This article lists confirmed features on the Yoma development roadmap that are relevant to deployers, opportunity partners, and reward partners. It also notes features that have been requested but are not currently planned, so you can set expectations with your stakeholders accurately.

Features listed here reflect what is confirmed as of February 2026. Timelines are subject to change — contact the Yoma team for the latest status on any item.

---

## Confirmed Roadmap Items

### Partner Navigation Menu Update — Q4 2026

The partner-facing navigation will be updated to improve access to the dashboard and opportunity management tools. Currently the partner portal navigation requires extra steps to reach common functions. This update addresses the layout and accessibility of those key areas, reducing friction for Organisation Admins managing active programmes.

**Who it affects:** Organisation Admins at opportunity partner organisations.

---

### Dashboard Data Download (CSV Export) — Q4 2026

Partners will be able to download their opportunity and engagement data directly from the partner dashboard as a CSV export. Currently, dashboard data is viewable on screen only — there is no export functionality. This is one of the most requested features from partners who need to feed data into their own reporting and monitoring systems.

**What will be exportable:** Opportunity applicants, completion data, and engagement metrics.

**Who it affects:** Organisation Admins and implementing organisations who rely on Yoma data for M&E reporting to funders or government.

**Note:** Until this feature is live, data collection for reporting purposes needs to be managed through supplementary methods. See [Platform Limitations](07-03-platform-limitations.md).

---

### Automated Verification of Certificates and Images — Q1 2027

Currently, all completion verification is manual — Organisation Admins review and approve or reject each submission individually. The planned automated verification feature will enable the platform to check uploaded certificates or images against defined criteria without requiring human review for each submission.

This will significantly reduce the administrative burden on opportunity partners managing high-volume programmes, and should improve verification turnaround times at scale.

**Who it affects:** Opportunity partners with large participant cohorts and high submission volumes.

**Note:** Until this feature is live, batch verification is the recommended approach for managing volume efficiently. See [Verification Workflows](../03-opportunities/03-06-verification-workflows.md).

---

### Basic CV Creation and Download from YoID — Q1 2027

Youth will be able to generate and download a basic CV directly from their YoID profile, drawing on their verified credentials and skills tags to produce a structured document they can share with employers or institutions.

Currently, YoID data can be shared via a link for online verification, but there is no option to produce a portable document. This feature directly addresses the employment and livelihoods use case — giving youth a tangible, shareable output from their Yoma engagement.

**Who it affects:** Youth and any implementing organisation or partner whose value proposition includes employment pathways.

---

### Cash Equivalent Rewards via Chimoney — 2026

A third reward model is planned that allows youth to redeem Zlto for direct cash transfers, facilitated by Chimoney. Under this model, the implementing organisation funds a monetary amount to the Zlto team, who list a cash equivalent reward on the marketplace. When a youth redeems it, Chimoney handles the cash transfer directly.

This significantly expands the practical value of Zlto in markets where voucher-based or in-kind rewards have limited reach or relevance.

**Who it affects:** Implementing organisations and reward partners exploring cash-based incentive models.

**Current status:** Planned for 2026. See [Types of Rewards](../04-rewards/04-03-types-of-rewards.md) for the current reward models available now.

## Open Source Contributions

The Yoma codebase is open source (github.com/didx-xyz/yoma). It is feasible for deployment partners or technical stakeholders to contribute features in parallel to the existing roadmap — for example, to address a specific local need not covered by the confirmed items above. Any contribution requires scoping with the Yoma development team before work begins.

If a feature is critical to your deployment and is not on the confirmed roadmap, raise it directly with the Yoma team as early as possible to explore whether an open source contribution is a viable path.

## Not on the Roadmap

The following are features that have been requested or discussed but are not currently planned for development. They are listed here to help you set accurate expectations with your partners and stakeholders.

| Feature                                               | Status                                        |
| ----------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Gamification (badges, leaderboards, achievements)     | Not on roadmap                                |
| Custom user roles                                     | Not on roadmap                                |
| Offline mode                                          | Not on roadmap                                |
| WhatsApp Youth CRM integration                        | Not on roadmap (feature request noted)        |
| Push notifications for new opportunities              | Not on roadmap (feature request noted)        |
| Parental consent mechanism for under-18 registrations | Not on roadmap — must be managed off-platform |
| Native mobile app                                     | Not on roadmap                                |

If any of these represent a significant requirement for your deployment, discuss them with the Yoma team. Some — particularly offline mode and WhatsApp integration — may be addressable through workarounds or third-party tools at the ecosystem level, even if not built into the platform.
