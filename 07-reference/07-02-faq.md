# FAQ

*Last updated: February 2026*

Frequently asked questions organised by audience. For definitions of any terms used here, see the [Glossary](07-01-glossary.md).

---

## For Implementing Organisations and Deployers

**What does it cost to deploy Yoma?**
The platform is free for both youth and partners. Yoma charges no licensing or usage fees. Your costs are operational: the team and time needed to build the local ecosystem, recruit partners, and support youth. Reward procurement may involve spend depending on your model (e.g., purchasing voucher codes from reward partners).

**What does Yoma / RLabs provide, and what do we have to build?**
Yoma provides the platform (hosted and maintained), technical support, bug resolution, System Administrator access, and the global partner community. The implementing organisation builds everything local: the opportunity partner network, the rewards catalogue, the youth base, the verification infrastructure, and the ongoing communications and operational management. The platform is ready; the ecosystem is not — that is the implementing organisation's job. See [How Yoma Is Deployed](../01-overview/01-04-how-yoma-is-deployed.md).

**Can we customise the platform for our market?**
Yoma runs as a single shared global platform — there are no bespoke builds per market. Customisation options are limited: you can configure what you list (opportunities and rewards), but you cannot change the platform interface, user flows, or roles. The codebase is open source, so it is theoretically possible to contribute new features, but any development work requires scoping with the Yoma team. See [Platform Limitations](07-03-platform-limitations.md).

**Who owns the youth data collected on the platform?**
Youth own their own data — Yoma uses a Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI) model. Individual credential records belong to each young person and cannot be accessed or revoked by the implementing organisation, partner, or Yoma without the youth's consent. Anonymised, aggregated data can be used for monitoring and evaluation purposes. The platform is GDPR-aligned by design.

**What languages is the platform available in?**
English, French, Kiswahili, Portuguese, and Arabic.

**What are the technical requirements for youth to access the platform?**
Any internet-enabled device with a browser. There is no native mobile app and no offline mode. Youth access yoma.world directly.

**Can we run a pilot before a full deployment?**
Yes, and it is recommended. Access to a staging environment for pre-launch testing needs to be arranged with the Yoma team — it is not self-service. If a staging environment is not available, testing can be conducted on the live platform with a small controlled group. See [Staging and Testing](../02-getting-started/02-05-staging-and-testing.md).

**How long does it take from decision to launch?**
This depends on how quickly you can build the ecosystem — opportunity partners, reward partners, and youth acquisition infrastructure must all be in place before a meaningful launch. The platform setup itself (registering your organisation, creating the first opportunities) can be done in days. Building the ecosystem typically takes weeks to months, depending on existing relationships and local context.

**Is there formal training or onboarding support from Yoma / RLabs?**
There is currently no formal onboarding documentation or training programme from Yoma / RLabs — this is a known gap. Yoma's recommended model is for the implementing organisation to take on the partner onboarding function locally, using a train-the-trainer approach. See [Partner Support Model](../06-operations/06-02-partner-support-model.md).

## For Opportunity Partners

**How do we register on Yoma?**
Navigate to yoma.world/organisations, upload your company registration document, complete your organisation profile, and submit. Applications are reviewed within 48 business hours. On approval, the submitting person receives Organisation Admin access. See [Setting Up Your Organisation](../02-getting-started/02-02-setting-up-your-organisation.md).

**How does verification work?**
There are three methods. Manual review: youth upload evidence and you approve or reject each submission through the partner portal. Open verification link / QR code: you generate a link or QR code that any youth can scan or click to auto-claim the credential — best for in-person events. Email-targeted verification link: you input a list of specific email addresses, and only Yoma accounts registered to those emails can open the link — useful when you have a confirmed attendee list from an RSVP form or learner management system. See [Verification Workflows](../03-opportunities/03-06-verification-workflows.md).

**How quickly should we verify submissions?**
The benchmark is 48 hours. In existing Yoma deployments, youth engagement drops significantly when verification takes longer than a week. Build internal capacity — or share admin access across team members — so the queue is reviewed regularly.

**What happens when we approve a completion?**
Two things happen automatically: Zlto tokens are issued to the youth's balance (initially appearing as "pending" for up to 24 hours while the system processes the transaction), and a Verifiable Credential is added to the youth's YoID. No manual action is required after approval.

**Can we reject a submission and ask the youth to resubmit?**
Yes. When rejecting a submission, you provide a reason. The youth receives a notification with that reason and can resubmit corrected evidence.

**How do we set the Zlto value for an opportunity?**
Zlto values are set by the partner at listing creation and can be edited after publication. Use the reference value of 100 Zlto ≈ 5 USD as your calibration point. Low-effort activities (under 30 minutes) typically sit at around 50 Zlto; significant time contributions (several hours) at 300–500 Zlto. See [Setting Zlto Values](../03-opportunities/03-05-setting-zloto-values.md).

**Can we export our data from the dashboard?**
Not currently. The partner dashboard is viewable but data cannot be downloaded or exported. CSV export functionality is on the development roadmap for Q4 2026. See [Roadmap Features](07-04-roadmap-features.md).

**What credential does a youth receive on completion?**
All opportunities — regardless of type — issue a Verifiable Credential using the default schema. The credential records who completed it, which opportunity, which organisation issued it, when it was verified, and which skills tags were attached. The opportunity type (Learning, Volunteering / Impact, or Employment / Livelihoods) is included in the credential data. Skills tags are the primary mechanism for differentiating credential content across different activities. See [Credentials and Skills](../03-opportunities/03-07-credentials-and-skills.md).

**Can we generate a link to share a specific opportunity directly with youth?**
Yes. After publishing an opportunity, you can generate a deep link to that listing. Sharing the link via WhatsApp, social media, or notice boards takes youth directly to the opportunity without them needing to search the marketplace.

## For Reward Partners

**How do we get our reward listed on the marketplace?**
Reward listings are not self-service. The process is: your organisation agrees terms with the implementing organisation; the implementing organisation procures voucher codes (if applicable) or confirms the reward details; those details are passed to the Zlto team (zlto.co), who create and manage the listing on the marketplace. See [Working with the Zlto Team](../04-rewards/04-04-working-with-the-zloto-team.md).

**Who manages our listing once it is live?**
The Zlto team. Reward partners do not have direct access to edit listings on the platform. Stock replenishment, pricing changes, and listing updates all go through the Zlto team via the implementing organisation.

**How is the Zlto price for our reward decided?**
The implementing organisation and reward partner agree a fair value, using the reference rate of 100 Zlto ≈ 5 USD to calibrate. The Zlto team advise on pricing that works within the broader rewards ecosystem. See [Pricing Rewards in Zlto](../04-rewards/04-05-pricing-rewards-in-zloto.md).

**What happens when our stock runs out?**
The listing shows as out of stock and disappears from the active catalogue until stock is refreshed. The implementing organisation manages the replenishment process with the reward partner and coordinates a refresh with the Zlto team.

**Do we need a formal contract with Yoma or the Zlto team?**
Typically the implementing organisation holds the relationship with the reward partner, and the Zlto team manages the platform side. Formal contractual arrangements depend on the market and the reward model. In some markets (e.g., South Africa), the Zlto team holds direct relationships with some reward partners.

## For Youth

**How do I sign up?**
Go to yoma.world, click "Log in," then "I don't have an account." You can sign up using your email, SMS, or via Google, Facebook, or Goodwall single sign-on. After confirming your account, complete your profile — first name, last name, country, education, gender, and date of birth. The whole process takes under five minutes. Your YoID is created automatically when your profile is complete.

**How do I earn Zlto?**
Browse the opportunity marketplace, find an activity that interests you, complete it, and submit your proof of completion. Once the partner organisation approves your submission, Zlto is issued to your account. It may initially show as "pending" — this is normal and will become available to spend within 24 hours.

**Why is my Zlto showing as "pending"?**
After a partner approves your completion, there is a short system processing period before the Zlto moves from "pending" to available in your balance. This is automated — it does not require any action from you or the partner. It typically resolves within 24 hours.

**How do I redeem a reward?**
Navigate to the rewards marketplace from the main Yoma menu. Browse the available rewards and find one you want. If your available Zlto balance meets the reward's price, you can redeem it — your Zlto is deducted immediately. How you receive the reward depends on the reward type: voucher codes are delivered automatically; other types (such as event RSVPs) are confirmed separately.

**Can I save up Zlto across multiple opportunities?**
Yes. Your Zlto balance accumulates across all your completions. You can save up over time to reach the threshold for higher-value rewards.

**What is YoID and what can I do with it?**
YoID is your Yoma Digital Identity — a portable, blockchain-recorded profile of everything you have verified on Yoma. Every approved opportunity adds a Verifiable Credential to your YoID. You own it and control who can access it. You can share a link to your YoID with employers, universities, or other institutions, giving them a verifiable record of your skills and experience without needing to rely on an unverifiable CV.

**Who can see my credentials?**
You control access to your YoID. Credentials are not automatically visible to anyone — you choose to share them by providing a link. Third parties with your link can verify the authenticity of any credential on your record.

**I've changed my mind about sharing my credentials. Can I remove them?**
Credentials issued to your YoID are permanent — once issued, they cannot be revoked or removed by the partner, Yoma, or the implementing organisation. The blockchain record is designed to be tamper-proof. You can choose not to share your YoID link with anyone, but the credentials themselves remain on your record.
