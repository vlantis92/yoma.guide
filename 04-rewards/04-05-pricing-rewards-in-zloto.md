# Pricing Rewards in Zlto

*Last updated: February 2026*

Reward pricing is where your points economy becomes real. Set prices well and youth feel that their effort translates into something tangible. Set them poorly â€” too high, too low, or inconsistently â€” and the Zlto motivation collapses. ## The Core Calculation

The starting point for pricing any reward is straightforward:

1. Establish the real-world value of the reward in USD (or your local currency converted to USD).
2. Apply the reference conversion: 100 Zlto = 5 USD, or 20 Zlto per USD.
3. Use the result as a baseline Zlto price.
4. Adjust up or down based on strategic considerations (see below).

## The Effort-to-Reward Question

Once you have a baseline price, ask: how many opportunities does a youth need to complete to earn this reward?

Given typical opportunity Zlto values (see [Setting Zlto Values](../03-opportunities/03-05-setting-zloto-values.md) for the full range), a 200 Zlto reward is accessible after a handful of low-to-medium effort activities. A 1,000 Zlto reward requires sustained engagement over weeks or months.

Both ends of this spectrum serve a purpose:

- **Low-threshold rewards** (50â€“300 Zlto) are important for new users â€” they give youth an early win and prove the system works. Make sure you have something accessible in this range at launch.
- **Mid-range rewards** (300â€“800 Zlto) sustain ongoing engagement. They're achievable with consistent participation over a few weeks.
- **Aspirational rewards** (800 Zlto+) create longer-term motivation and retention. University scholarship access is the classic example â€” a youth who knows they're working towards a scholarship registration threshold will stay active for months.

## Adjusting for Strategic Fit

The base calculation is a starting point. Adjust the Zlto price upward or downward based on:

**Adjust up if:** The reward is high-demand and you need to manage redemption volume against limited supply. A higher Zlto price naturally paces demand without you having to manually limit stock as frequently.

**Adjust down if:** You want to use the reward as an entry-point hook â€” something that converts first-time users into active participants. A deliberately accessible first reward can be worth subsidising slightly below fair value.

**Adjust to match your opportunity mix:** If your opportunity portfolio skews heavily toward low-Zlto activities (50â€“150 Zlto), make sure your rewards catalogue has plenty of options in the 200â€“400 Zlto range â€” otherwise youth will accumulate Zlto with no immediate redemption path, and motivation stalls.

## Coordinating Prices Across the Ecosystem

Reward pricing cannot be set in isolation. It needs to be coordinated with:

- **The Zlto team** â€” who create and manage listings. Agree the Zlto price with them before the listing goes live. Don't assume they'll set a price on your behalf.
- **Your opportunity partners** â€” if partners are setting their own Zlto values, review them against your reward prices. A partner who sets every opportunity at 10 Zlto will frustrate youth who can see rewards starting at 200 Zlto.
- **Your implementing organisation's M&E goals** â€” if funders want to see specific engagement levels, reward pricing directly affects participation rates.

## Reviewing and Adjusting

Reward prices aren't permanent. Once your deployment is live, watch redemption data. Rewards with very high redemption rates relative to supply may need a price increase to manage stock. Rewards that rarely get redeemed may be priced too high, or simply not relevant to your youth demographic.

Build a pricing review into your quarterly operational rhythm. Coordinate any changes with the Zlto team â€” listing updates go through them, as covered in [Working with the Zlto Team](04-04-working-with-the-zloto-team.md).
